Leilani H. Gilpin

PhD Candidate

Massachusetts Institute of Technology

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT in CSAIL. My research focuses on the theories and methodologies towards monitoring, designing, and augmenting machines that can explain themselves for diagnosis, accountability, and liability. My long-term research vision is for self-explaining, intelligent, machines by design. During my PhD, I have developed local monitoring frameworks to explain opaque mechanims, towards a full-system that uses explanations for more robust decision making. This allows machines and other complex mechanisms to be able to interpret their actions and learn from their mistakes.

I am on the academic job market this year. My materials are available: research statement, teaching statement, diversity statement, curriculum vitae
